About Providential Mathematics Olympiads

The Providential Mathematics Olympiads are a series of olympiad-level contests in mathematics. The main focus of the olympiads are not on breadth of mathematical knowledge and techniques, but instead focused on a competitor's ability to think creatively to solve difficult problems under an intense environment. Competitor's will find that all of the problems can be solved easily using techniques and principles introduced in secondary school, however it is difficult to devise those solutions by themselves.


Although olympiad-style examinations are usually used to select top performing students for national teams and competition in international competitions, the intensity and problem-solving nature of math olympiads makes it especially effective in cultivating math and problem-solving skills in all young mathematicians. We believe that if used correctly, math olympiads can be used as valuable learning tools to develop much more than math skills.<\p>
